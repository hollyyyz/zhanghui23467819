Building a cultural bridge -- Mainland people’s life stories in Hong Kong

Holly Zhang

May 1

In Hong Kong, a multicultural city, many mainlanders have taken root in this bustling city. They are gradually adapting to the new environment, learning the local language and integrating into local life. Through the experiences of three different individuals, we will deeply explore the life stories of mainlanders in Hong Kong, the challenges they face in different cultural backgrounds, and explore the process of cross-cultural integration.

A property manager’s journey of struggle in Hong Kong

Aunt Li was born in Zhejiang Province. She followed her children to Hong Kong two years ago and started a new chapter in her life. This choice marked a major turning point in her life. The child lives in Shenzhen. To pursue better opportunities, Aunt Li decided to go to Hong Kong with her relatives and return to Shenzhen to accompany the child in her spare time. However, when she first arrived, she faced the dual challenge of a language barrier and an unfamiliar environment and encountered obstacles at every turn. Despite the difficulties, Aunt Li did not give up and bravely faced the difficult challenge of adapting to the new culture. She found a job as a property manager in a community dominated by mainland students.

Every morning, Aunt Li starts her busy work in the community. She sticks to the parking lot and entrance, often working the night shift, and sometimes even working until six o'clock the next morning. Although she is sixty years old, she still perseveres and silently struggles for life and future. In her spare time, she continues to work hard to learn Cantonese to better integrate into the life of this city. During her break, she would return to Shenzhen to reunite with her children and provide them with care and companionship. After a year of hard work, Aunt Li gradually became familiar with life in Hong Kong and became comfortable at work. However, she does not stop there, but sees herself as a member of the residential community, not just as an administrator, but as a cultural bridge. She uses her actions to promote understanding and harmony among residents from different backgrounds and has become a flag in the community.

The integration of cross-cultural marriage and career

By chance, we met Daisy, a student from Zhuhai. Studying for degree at Hong Kong Baptist University. After studying, she worked in a local cafe. Since she grew up in Guangdong, there was no language barrier for her. She gradually fell in love with the city of Hong Kong and enjoyed the beauty of city life. She decided to stay in Hong Kong after graduation and work in this city. The city found its feet.

Through communicating with customers from different cultural backgrounds, Ms. Daisy discovered the beauty of cross-cultural dialogue and communication. As she immersed herself in the rhythms of Hong Kong's streets, she discovered the importance of education and personal growth in the complex process of cultural integration. The profound impact of cross-cultural experiences in shaping personal identity and promoting mutual understanding.

Academic exploration and cultural exchange

Aunt Lam, who met her current husband in the Mainland more than 20 years ago, decided to leave Fujian and follow him to Hong Kong. A cross-cultural wedding banquet took her future life in a completely different direction. Since her husband's family has been running a beef brisket noodle shop in Wan Chai for generations since 1955, she helped her husband in his shop.

At first, she could not speak Cantonese, and there was a language barrier with her mother-in-law, and she was not used to living together. For the sake of her family and herself, she worked hard to learn Cantonese. She learned it in only half a year and was praised by her mother-in-law. Now their son is studying abroad, and she helps out as a front-desk worker at a noodle shop. With the joint efforts of her husband and his family, the business is very prosperous.

Many Hong Kong tycoons and celebrities come to patronize their shop, and Auntie Lam has adapted to it. Living in the local area, she is outgoing and open-minded, unlike her introverted husband. She has established deep friendships with many neighbors and consolidated her customer base. She also integrated Hong Kong's unique food culture with that of her hometown of Fujian to design some unique dishes. She earned respect and recognition on her own, and continued to write a new chapter in her life through her efforts in this land.

These three stories show the life trajectory of mainlanders in Hong Kong, as well as the challenges and opportunities they face in cross-cultural integration. Whether it’s Aunt Li trying to adapt to life in Hong Kong and becoming a cultural bridge to the community, or Daisy Wang discovering the importance of personal growth through academic exploration and cultural exchanges, or Aunt Lam winning respect and recognition in the integration of cross-cultural marriage and career. , all show the diversity and richness of life of mainlanders in Hong Kong.

Crossing cultural barriers requires courage and perseverance, but it will also bring broader development space and life experience. Through mutual understanding, respect and cooperation, the life experience of mainlanders in Hong Kong will continue to be enriched and deepened, becoming an important part of this multicultural city.
